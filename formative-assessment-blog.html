Making Learning Visible: How I Use Formative Assessment in My Classroom

Formative assessment turns thinking into something we can see, discuss, and improve together. Below are two hands-on activities that help my students connect algorithms to real-world action.

By Gracielle Oliveira Mota Dias — IB Computer Science & Design Innovation • Pan American School of Bahia
IB DP Computational Thinking Project-Based Learning

The “Sandwich Algorithm”

Students write precise, step-by-step instructions (an algorithm), then swap and execute each other’s “code” using real ingredients.

  1. Divide the class into two teams; provide ingredients and utensils.
  2. Each team writes a step-by-step algorithm to assemble a sandwich.
  3. Teams swap algorithms and follow them exactly.
  4. Debrief: Where did ambiguity create “bugs”? How would you refactor?

Skills: clarity, sequencing, decomposition, testing.

The Real-Life Maze Challenge

A physical maze in the classroom with team roles mirrors a live development workflow.

  • Programmer: drafts the command sequence.
  • Debugger: finds and fixes logic errors.
  • Executor: runs the program to navigate the maze.

Skills: iteration, debugging, teamwork, resilience.

What Works — and What Stretches Us

Successes
  • High engagement and deeper conceptual understanding.
  • Natural collaboration and peer teaching emerge.
  • Assessment becomes authentic, timely, and actionable.
Challenges
  • Preparation and classroom setup take time and space.
  • Managing different paces requires adaptive facilitation.
  • Outcomes are open-ended — teachers must embrace iteration.

Final Reflection

Formative assessment is a continuous dialogue. It celebrates curiosity, creativity, and growth—and reminds us that learning is something we build together, step by step (and sometimes, bite by bite).
